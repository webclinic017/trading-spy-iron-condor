## Core Philosophy and Principles

Phil Town's approach to options trading emphasizes **strategic investing rather than speculation**. The fundamental philosophy integrates options into a broader value-investing framework focused on "wonderful companies at an on-sale price."

### Key Foundational Beliefs

- Options should generate income and reduce risk, not create excessive exposure
- Portfolios following this methodology have "never experienced a down year" over a decade of trading
- Discipline and education matter more than chasing high returns
- Options work best when aligned with long-term wealth building
- "If you wouldn't want to own a company for 10 years, you shouldn't own it for 10 minutes"

## Primary Trading Strategies

### A. Selling Put Options (The Rule #1 Put)

**Purpose:** Generate income while acquiring stocks at predetermined favorable prices

**Execution Process:**
1. Identify a "wonderful company" worthy of long-term ownership
2. Determine the target "on-sale" price point
3. Sell put options at that strike price
4. Collect premium upfront regardless of price movement

**Example:**
- Stock trading at $100, investor wants entry at $90
- Sell put option at $90 strike
- **If stock falls to $90:** Forced purchase at target price (desired outcome)
- **If stock stays above $90:** Keep premium, no purchase obligation

**Income Benefit:** "Collect a premium received upfront, earning money regardless of whether the underlying asset's price reaches the target price or not"

**Phil's Approach to OTM Puts:**
- Main reason: Reduce cost basis or increase position size
- Uses long-term contracts (LEAPS)
- Takes advantage of increased Implied Volatility during uncertainty
- Example: CF Industries position at $190, sold LEAPS puts down to $160s to lower basis

**Critical Warning:** Phil expresses skepticism about naked puts because "Mr. Market can crash prices far below your margin of safety level"

---

### B. Selling Call Options (The Rule #1 Call/Covered Call)

**Purpose:** Generate additional returns on already-owned positions

**Execution Process:**
1. Sell call options on stocks currently owned
2. Earn immediate premium income
3. If stock rises above strike: sell shares at profit
4. If stock stays below strike: retain stock plus premium

**Example:**
- Own stock at $90, believe it may reach $110
- Sell calls at $110 strike
- **If stock reaches $110:** Profitable sale occurs
- **If stock remains below $110:** Keep both stock and collected premium

**Phil's Perspective:** "Rule #1 investors love selling call options when they already own the stock, as there is virtually no risk, and they get more money for selling stock they would have sold anyway"

**Example from Blog:**
- Own 100 ABC shares at $108
- Sell a $115 call for $0.37 premium
- Receive $37 regardless of outcome

## Income Generation Framework

The strategy treats options as an income-generating tool with two complementary approaches:

1. **Selling Puts:** Target entry into wonderful companies at discount prices while collecting income
2. **Selling Covered Calls:** Extract additional returns from existing positions while preparing profitable exits

This dual approach creates "a steady stream of income" without requiring outright stock ownership through put sales alone.

## Risk Management and Protection

### Core Risk Principles

The methodology emphasizes **relatively low-risk strategies** that differ fundamentally from speculative approaches. Key protective mechanisms include:

- **Predefined Entry Points:** Selling puts only at predetermined "on-sale" prices ensures disciplined acquisition
- **Downside Protection:** "Reduces downside risk by ensuring investments are made at pre-determined, favorable prices"
- **Capital Preservation:** Unlike speculation, the approach focuses on "reducing risk, helping retirees protect their nest egg"
- **Understand intrinsic business value thoroughly before trading**
- **Know what you're doing—options can be risky if misunderstood**
- **Recognize how overall market conditions affect outcomes**

### Avoiding Speculative Traps

Town explicitly warns against high-risk strategies like long straddles and strangles. The methodology prioritizes "consistent income and risk management rather than speculative gains."

**Why 90% of Option Traders Lose Money:**
Town attributes losses to traders who "use high-risk speculative strategies, trade without a clear plan, or fail to manage downside risk and additional costs."

### The 7% Rule

General risk management concept where investors "sell a stock if it drops 7% below their purchase price to limit losses"

## Recommended Advanced Strategy: The Collar

**Collar Strategy:** Buying puts while selling calls simultaneously

**Benefits:**
- Limits downside risk
- Preserves upside potential
- Provides defined risk parameters

## Volatility Considerations

Market volatility significantly impacts option premium pricing:

- **High volatility:** Creates higher premiums, favoring sellers
- **Low volatility:** Results in lower premiums
- **Rule #1 advantage:** "Sell options when premiums are high, thereby maximizing income"

**Key Insight:** Phil takes advantage of increased Implied Volatility created by market uncertainty to increase premiums on puts with strike prices low enough that he'd be happy to own the business at that price

Investors should analyze "market conditions and volatility trends" before executing trades to optimize premium collection.

## Exit and Position Management

### Call Option Exits:
- Allow assignment if stock appreciates above strike (profit realization)
- If stock remains below strike at expiration, retain ownership and premium

### Put Option Exits:
- Accept assignment if stock falls to strike price (acquire at target)
- Allow expiration worthless if stock stays above strike (premium retention)
