RAG Retrieval Flow: How Past Lessons Inform Current Decisions

A horizontal process flow diagram showing how Retrieval-Augmented Generation works in the trading system.

The flow moves left to right through 6 stages, connected by arrows:

Stage 1 — "New Situation":
A box showing an example input: "Should we open a SPY iron condor with earnings next week?"
Annotation: "A question or decision point arrives"

Stage 2 — "Embed Query":
The input text is converted into a numerical vector. Show a small visual of [0.23, -0.41, 0.87, ...] representing a 768-dimensional embedding.
Annotation: "Text transformed into 768-dimensional vector using sentence-transformers"

Stage 3 — "Semantic Search":
A cylinder representing the LanceDB vector store with "300+ lessons" inside. Show multiple vectors inside the database with distance lines connecting to the query vector. The closest matches are highlighted.
Annotation: "Cosine similarity finds the most relevant past lessons"

Stage 4 — "Top-K Results":
Show 3 retrieved lesson cards:
- "LL-268: Exit at 7 DTE to avoid gamma risk (similarity: 0.92)"
- "LL-220: 15-delta = 86% win rate (similarity: 0.85)"
- "LL-198: Never hold through earnings (similarity: 0.83)"
Annotation: "Most relevant lessons ranked by similarity"

Stage 5 — "Context Injection":
A document showing the LLM prompt with the retrieved lessons prepended: "RELEVANT LESSONS: [LL-268, LL-220, LL-198] + Original Question"
Annotation: "Past knowledge injected into the prompt before the LLM responds"

Stage 6 — "Informed Decision":
Output box: "DO NOT open iron condor — earnings next week violates LL-198. Wait until after earnings report."
Annotation: "Decision made with full historical context"

Below the flow, a feedback arrow loops back from Stage 6 to Stage 3: "Result stored as new lesson, enriching future retrievals"

Style: clean, modern process diagram with rounded boxes, soft blue/teal color palette, white background, professional typography. Not a system architecture diagram — this shows the conceptual process of how knowledge flows.
