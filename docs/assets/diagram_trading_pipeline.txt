End-to-End Trading Pipeline: SPY Iron Condor Execution

The trading pipeline processes a SPY iron condor trade through six sequential stages with safety gates at each step.

Stage 1 - Thompson Sampler:
Selects the optimal strategy using Beta-Bernoulli distributions. Each strategy (iron condor, credit spread, etc.) has a win/loss distribution. The sampler draws from these distributions to pick the highest-probability strategy. Currently SPY iron condors at 15-20 delta.

Stage 2 - Trade Memory Query:
Before entering any trade, the system queries a SQLite database of all past trades. Pattern matching identifies: "This exact setup (SPY IC, 15-delta, 35 DTE) has been traded 12 times with 83% win rate." Past failures with similar setups trigger warnings.

Stage 3 - RAG Knowledge Retrieval:
LanceDB semantic search over 300+ documented lessons learned. Queries like "iron condor risk near earnings" retrieve relevant lessons (e.g., LL-268: exit at 7 DTE not 21 DTE to avoid gamma risk). Retrieved context is injected into the decision pipeline.

Stage 4 - Risk Manager:
Enforces hard limits: maximum 5% of account ($5,000) per position, maximum 2 concurrent iron condors, $10-wide wings only. Stop-loss at 100% of credit received is mandatory with no exceptions.

Stage 5 - LLM Council (via TARS):
Multiple models vote on the trade. DeepSeek-R1 provides chain-of-thought reasoning on entry timing. Kimi K2 (StockBench #1) analyzes the options structure. Consensus required before execution. All calls routed through TARS gateway.

Stage 6 - Alpaca Execution:
Paper trading account PA3C5AG0CECQ ($100K). 4-leg order submitted: sell put spread (bull put) + sell call spread (bear call). Exit targets: 50% profit OR 7 DTE, whichever comes first.

Post-Trade: Results feed back into Thompson Sampler (Stage 1) and Trade Memory (Stage 2), closing the learning loop.
